The Experiment

How we tested — methodology first

What we tested

  • React invoice form with 10 predefined business rules
  • Same rules, same models, different prompt formats
  • Rules: VAT logic, blocked countries, retry policy, analytics, autosave…
  • Judge: deterministic TypeScript — zero AI in scoring

Models tested

  • mistral 7b instruct — local, free
  • codeqwen 7b — local, code-focused
  • gemini 2.5 flash — commercial
  • claude sonnet — commercial
  • Dashboard: text noise analysis (T4)
Score = how many of the 10 business rules appeared correctly in the output. Like unit tests — explicit assertions, deterministic check.

Conclusion: You don't need a standard. You don't need a schema validator. Pick any format that makes your intent explicit — structure is sufficient.

S · I · F · Y
The Spec Is For You — a thinking framework for AI-assisted development
S

Structure before generation

Write the spec before the prompt. Like TDD — test before code. The spec is a contract that forces your implicit knowledge into explicit form.

→ proven T1: 2/10 → 9/10 from format alone
I

Intent is yours, not AI's

AI knows patterns. It does not know your business. No model will invent your domain rules — they live only in your head until you write them down.

→ proven T6: Claude 1306 tokens → 0/10
F

Format over syntax

Structure matters. Key names don't. vat_in and vat_eu_pct produce identical results. No standard needed — just structure.

→ proven T7: weird keys scored 10/10
Y

Your assumed is your risk

Every silent decision is invisible until it breaks. assumed: 0 doesn't mean zero assumptions — it means they weren't reported.

→ proven T8: assumed 10 → 0 with comments
Antipatterns

Four ways to look in control — while you're not

Prompt and pray
violates S — structure first
Iterate without a contract. Each prompt shifts the context. By message five you've forgotten what you wanted and the model optimizes for your last message — not your first intent.
Spec delegation
violates I — intent is yours
Ask AI to write the spec. Get beautiful, well-structured YAML — with zero of your business rules. Claude spent 1,306 tokens producing a spec that scored 0/10 on domain requirements.
Schema paralysis
violates F — format over syntax
Spend a day researching the "correct" YAML format instead of writing the spec. T7 proved: the model doesn't care about key names. Pick any format and start writing.
Silent success ⚠
violates Y — your assumed is your risk
Code works. Tests pass. Ten decisions were made without you — embedded in generated code, invisible until the German customer tries to pay on a Tuesday afternoon.
